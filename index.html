Constitution

NEMSS 2014 Set Constitution

Article I: Name and Purpose

On no account shall any member edit this Constitution except where a formal need for amendment arises. Once adopted, this Constitution supersedes all previous rules and regulations and may only be amended after four (4) years from the date of adoption.

Section A: Name

Part 1: The official name of this Union, effective from the date this Constitution is adopted, shall be New Era Model Secondary School

Part 2: The Union shall be known and referred to in all publicity materials and official correspondence as NEMSS 2014 SET

Section B: Purpose

Part 1: The purpose of this Constitution is to establish clear guidelines, rules, and expectations governing members‚Äô conduct, interactions, and decision-making processes within the Union. It serves as a framework to maintain order, promote discipline, facilitate effective communication, and foster a positive and respectful environment. The Constitution further outlines membership requirements, administrative procedures, dispute resolution mechanisms, and other relevant regulations necessary for the smooth operation and sustainability of the Union.

Part 2: All activities, programs, and decisions of the Union shall be strictly directed toward achieving and upholding the stated purpose of this Constitution.

Article II: Membership

Section A: Requirements

Part 1: All members of the Union must be ex-students of New Era Model Secondary School, whether or not they wrote WAEC at the school.

Part 2: All members must have their personal identity information (PII) duly registered with the Union.

Part 3: All members are required to demonstrate commitment by paying mandatory monthly dues.

Part 4: Membership decisions shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, age, medical condition, marital status, or citizenship.

Part 5: All members are mandated to support and participate in any approved contribution or financial obligation of the Union.

Section B: Rights

Part 1: All members are eligible to vote during the election of executives and on other matters formally presented to the Union by the executive body.

Part 2: All active and financially compliant members are entitled to receive benefits and support provided by the Union when the need arises.

Part 3: All members have the right to invite the Union to events or functions of personal or collective significance.

Section C: Withdrawal of Membership

Part One: A member‚Äôs membership may be withdrawn for failure to comply with the membership requirements outlined in Section A of this Article.

Part Two: Withdrawal of membership shall require a simple majority vote of a quorum of members present at a regular or special meeting.

Part Three: Any member subject to a proposed withdrawal shall be notified in writing at least one (1) week prior to the meeting at which the vote will be taken.

Article III: Officers and Governance

Regular meetings shall be held monthly to discuss community matters, share memories, and strengthen bonds. Special gatherings may be organized for celebrations, remembrances, and community service. All members will receive adequate notice of meetings and are encouraged to attend and participate actively.

Section C: Duties

Part One ‚Äì The Chairman: Holds the central executive authority of the Union and oversees its overall affairs. The Chairman may suspend any executive member found guilty of misconduct, subject to constitutional provisions.

Part Two ‚Äì The Vice-Chairman: Assists the Chairman and assumes leadership responsibilities in the absence of the Chairman.

Part Three ‚Äì The Secretary: Responsible for documenting Union activities and preparing all official records and correspondence

Part Four ‚Äì The Financial Secretary: Oversees policies relating to the inflow and outflow of all financial resources of the Union

Part Five ‚Äì The Treasurer: Manages the financial administration of the Union, reviews financial procedures and records, and advises on financial strategy and fundraising

Part Six ‚Äì The Welfare Officer: Oversees welfare activities, provides necessary amenities, and supports the wellbeing of members

Part Seven ‚Äì The Provost: Enforces Union decisions, maintains order, and supports the organization of meetings and events.

Part Eight ‚Äì Chairman, Planning Committee: Ensures that all events organized or attended by the Union are properly planned and successfully executed

Part Nine ‚Äì Vice-Chairman, Planning Committee: Assists the Chairman of the Planning Committee and assumes duties in their absence

Section D: Requirements for Running and Holding Office

Part One: All candidates must be registered members of the Union

Part Two: Candidates must actively participate in Union activities.

Part Three: Candidates must not have any record that may negatively affect the Union.

Part Four: Candidates must be willing to serve without financial remuneration.

Part Five: All officers must be voting members of NEMSS 2014 SET

Part Six: The Chairman and Vice-Chairman of the Planning Committee shall be special appointments made by the Executive Chairman. Their appointments expire at the end of each tenure, and the Executive Chairman reserves the right to replace them.

Note: All executive members shall be addressed as Comrades

Section E: Nomination and Elections

Part One: Elections shall be conducted every two (2) years at the end of each tenure. Any executive member seeking re-election must formally indicate interest

Part Two: No individual may serve more than three (3) tenures as Chairman.

Part Three: Nominations shall commence one (1) month after elections.

Part Four: Any member may nominate a qualified and eligible member for an elective executive position.

Part Five: A simple majority vote of a quorum present on election day shall be sufficient to elect an officer. Where no candidate attains a majority, a run-off election shall be conducted between the two highest vote recipients.

Section F: Removal from Office

Part One: Officers may be removed from office for failure to perform assigned duties or for violation of membership provisions.

Part Two: Any officer may resign by submitting a written letter to the Executive body. In such cases, the Vice-Chairman shall assume leadership, and other offices shall be filled in accordance with Union procedures

Part Three: In cases of misconduct, including embezzlement of funds or related offenses, the Union shall invoke the provisions of this Constitution to suspend any executive member found guilty

Article IV: Meetings and Events

Section A: Meetings

The Union shall convene meetings as required to deliberate on important issues affecting its members and operations. Regular activities shall be organized to promote unity, accountability, and collective responsibility. All engagements conducted under the Union‚Äôs authority shall uphold integrity, mutual respect, and the shared values of the community.

Part One: The Union may hold meetings whenever important matters arise that require collective discussion or decision-making.

Part Two: The last Friday of every month shall be dedicated to prayers and spiritual reflection.

Part Three: Mondays shall be reserved for business advertisements among members to encourage mutual patronage and unity. No form of fraud or scam shall be tolerated; any defaulter shall be severely sanctioned.

Part Four: The Union shall support the less privileged through charitable initiatives, including visits to orphanages and motherless babies‚Äô homes, payment of school fees for those in need, and the provision of scholarships when necessary.

Section B: Events

Members may invite the Union to personal or communal events to promote unity and shared participation. Adequate notice is required to enable proper planning and coordination, except where otherwise stated.

General Requirement: Any member wishing to invite the Union to an event shall give at least one (1) month‚Äôs notice, except for events listed under Parts Five, Six, and Eight.

Part Two: Every member of the Union shall pay the sum of (#2,000) two thousand naira for any event mentioned in Article IV, Section E, Part One to support a member who is holding a wedding ceremony.

Part Three: Every member of the Union shall pay the sum of (#1,000) one thousand naira for any event mentioned in Article IV, Section E, Part Two to support a member dedicating his/her child.

Section A: Contributions

Part Four: Contributions are mandatory for every member as specified in Article II, Section A, Part Five

Section B: Supports

Part One: Weddings ‚Äì A fixed amount of (#50,000) fifty thousand naira shall be given to any member who invites the Union to their wedding ceremony.

Part Two: Burials ‚Äì A fixed amount of (#40,000) forty thousand naira shall be given to any member who loses a parent. Note: This applies only to fathers or mothers, not uncles, aunts, or other relatives.

Part Three: Deceased Members ‚Äì All funds collected for deceased members shall be given to the family of the member who has passed away.

Part Four: Child Dedication ‚Äì A fixed amount of (#30,000) thirty thousand naira shall be given to any member who invites the Union to a child dedication ceremony.

Part Five: Birthdays ‚Äì Every member celebrating a birthday shall receive greetings and recognition from the general membership of the Union.

Part Six: Party ‚Äì The Union shall organize an end-of-year get-together party to give thanks and celebrate achievements. Fundraising modalities shall be presented when necessary.

Section C: Monthly Dues

Note: Monthly dues may be paid monthly or yearly and shall be used for Union needs such as get-together parties, supporting less privileged homes, providing scholarships, or helping members in financial need.

Part One: Monthly dues are compulsory for every member of the Union and signify active membership.

Part Two: The sum of (#500) five hundred naira is to be paid every month as monthly dues.

Part Three: Failure to pay monthly dues shall attract penalties as determined by the Union.

Part Four: All monthly dues must be fully paid before the end of each calendar year.

Article VI: By-Laws, Punishments and Amendment

Section A: Laws

Part One: The use of abusive or offensive language is strictly prohibited within the Union. Any offender must submit an immediate apology letter to the affected party.

Part Two: Individual defamation of any member is not allowed. Offenders shall tender an apology letter and may face temporary suspension.

Part Three: Posting of nude or pornographic materials in the Union WhatsApp group is strictly forbidden. Violators will be heavily fined.

Part Four: Scamming or posting fake links within the Union WhatsApp group is prohibited.

Part Five: Members are not allowed to leave the Union WhatsApp group arbitrarily. A proper reason must be documented if a member chooses to leave.

Part Six: Embezzlement or misappropriation of Union funds is strictly forbidden and will lead to severe disciplinary action.

Section B: Punishments

Part One: Any offender causing harm or offense to another member shall write an apology letter to the affected party.

Part Two: Members who leave the Union WhatsApp group without prior notice shall be fined (#3,000) three thousand naira upon rejoining, or may be refused reentry.

Part Three: Any member posting pornographic or nude images in the group shall be fined (#5,000) five thousand naira.

Part Four: Members caught embezzling funds will be removed from the Union and reported to the relevant authorities.

Part Five: Members inviting the Union to an event who are not up to date with levies or monthly dues shall not receive attendance until all debts are cleared, including a twenty percent (20%) penalty on late monthly dues.

Part Six: Members who invite the Union to events but fail to contribute to others‚Äô events, even after paying monthly dues, shall not receive support or attendance for their events.

Article IX: Rights and Privileges

Every member has the right to contribute their memories, thoughts, and perspectives to the community archive. Members enjoy equal access to all community resources, voting rights on important matters, and the privilege of receiving support from fellow members during significant life events.
